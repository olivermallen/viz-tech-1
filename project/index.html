Cottagecore To Tradwife Pipeline

Demonstrated with hashtags on Tumblr

My dataset is a collection of posts from the popular blogging platform Tumblr. These posts have been collected based on hashtags, and the hashtags collected were “#cottagecore”, “#tradfem”, “#tradwife”, and “#tradblr”. This dataset was collected as part of another project to model the “Cottagecore to Tradwife Pipeline”, which is a potential route to extremism through overlapping imagery. Cottagecore is an internet-based aesthetic that revolves around a romanticized rural lifestyle, which is shared through social media postings. Traditional Feminists (Tradwives, Tradfems, etc) are a sect of conservative women who ascribe to conservative values, traditional gender roles, and often white supremacy. Both of these demographics post on Tumblr, and because they have overlapping interests like baking, homemaking, and gardening they can end up using the same hashtags. The consumption of content related to cottagecore can lead to accidentally stumbling into spaces where conservative women discuss their hobbies of canning, sewing, gardening as well as their political views, which is called the “Cottagecore to Tradwife Pipeline”.

In order to visualize this story I want to use the martini glass structure, which will allow me to tell an introductory story about the cottagecore to tradwife pipeline and then leave the visualization open to the reader to explore other related hashtags. I plan to use a consistent visual platform to bring unity to the visualization. Because I have information about post time I want to construct a line graph. This way I can show the popularity of different hashtags posted with tradwife content over time, including a timeline slider. I will start my visualization by showing how many tradwife blogs also post the cottagecore tag, and then I will leave it to the viewer to discover other potential pipelines. I would also like to add details on demand to allow viewers to navigate to specific blogs or posts to explore further. Lots of social media data visualizations are aggregated which can cause loss of detail/nuance, so this approach will allow viewers to actually read some of the content represented in the visual. I will use Markers of Interactivity to show how users can interact with the visualization. I want to make the legend a network of tags to show relationships between them.
